PLC & Robotics

Industrial automation as one applied mechatronics problem: sensing, actuation, and control integrated into working cells you can build, and debug when they fail.

The Allen-Bradley PLC and the MELFA SCARA arm are not the subject of this course. They are the testbed. What you learn here are the principles used across all industrial automation, and this hardware is where you make them real.

Every unit rehearses a principle in open-source simulation, then applies the same principle to a different task on real hardware. You are assessed on transfer and on troubleshooting, not on reproducing a memorized sequence.

Four units

The course, in sequence

01 Sense

Industrial Sensing

Detecting and interpreting system state. Proximity, photoelectric, and limit sensors; thresholds, false detection, wiring faults, and noisy signals.

Open unit →
02 Act

Industrial Actuation

Producing physical action. Motors, relays, solenoids, and the robot's servo axes; output-not-firing, wrong direction, timing mismatch, and unsafe sequence.

Open unit →
03 Decide

Industrial Control

Deciding and regulating behavior. PLC sequencing with timers, counters, interlocks, and fault handling; robot motion control as the continuous case.

Open unit →
04 Integrate

System Integration

Combining all three into a detect–decide–actuate–verify–handle-faults cell, with PLC–robot handshake and HMI-style status.
